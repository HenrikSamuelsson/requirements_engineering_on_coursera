### Introduction to the Specialization

The role of a **requirements engineer** is to act as a link between the customer and the development team. The requirements engineer must understand the system at a high level and translate the customer’s needs into clear, structured requirements that enable developers to build the correct system.

## Software Lifecycle

### The Software Lifecycle

A lifecycle is the series of stages an organism, product, or system passes through, from inception and growth to maturity, decline, or disposal.

Software development can follow a lifecycle with the stages:

- Requirements
- Design
- Implementation
- Testing
- Management

Companies will often further define the lifecycle as a process to be followed. These processes will often be based on existing models.

One example of a model is the waterfall model. The name comes from that water falling down mountains in nature will go down in sequential stages resting in ponds between several smaller falls. The water may circulate for a while in a pond but once it have fallen it can not go up again to an upper pond.

The thinking with processes that follow the waterfall model is that one step shall be fully completed before starting the next. In reality it can be hard to follow the waterfall model because the stakeholders understanding of the system will evolve during the later stages this may force rework of the earlier stages.
